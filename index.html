01 /

THE BACKSTORY & INSPIRATION

It all started with a simple question: Why does Netflix operate its online merchandise store as a separate, external site that is completely disconnected from the streaming app itself?

Netflix fans have massive emotional connections to characters and stories. When they are watching a show and see an iconic item—like Max's jacket or a Stranger Things prop—they want it at that exact moment. By forcing users to close the app, go to their browser, search for the item, and register on a different site, Netflix loses over 95% of potential impulse purchases.

I pitched this conceptual integration to close the gap. Over a few weeks, we designed a native mobile mockup, established consistent typography, and ran interactive user studies to build a high-fidelity prototype of the unified shopping experience.

02 /

THE CHALLENGE & TEAM STRATEGY

Being a passion-driven personal project, we had zero official resource allocation. Our engineering and design squads were fully swamped with regular client work. However, the sheer impact of the idea kept us inspired. We gathered after hours, met on weekends, and spent our team hackathons building and iterating the interactive prototype.

The core strategic challenge was balance. We had to implement shopping and social community features without distracting viewers or making the streaming app feel like a cluttered department store catalog. By focusing on in-scene tag targets and floating overlay icons, we created a system that is invisible during standard playback and reveals itself only upon direct user interest.

03 /

USER RESEARCH & A/B TESTING

Before putting pen to paper, we surveyed 150 active Netflix subscribers. The findings were staggering:

  • 72% of fans expressed immediate interest in buying clothing or props seen on their favorite shows.
  • 64% of users had no idea that Netflix even had an official external merchandise store (Netflix.shop).
  • 88% of users stated they would drop off if they had to register a separate account or input billing details manually to buy merch.

We built interactive high-fidelity A/B testing models. Test cohorts using our integrated in-stream design showed a dramatic reduction in search friction, vaulting product interest, and maintaining high platform session lengths since users read community reviews right inside the show’s dashboard.

01 /

THE BACKSTORY & INSPIRATION

It all started with a simple observation: engineers operating on distributed, active construction sites were spending hours organizing physical receipts instead of building.

Cendrol Construct is a construction and interior solutions company managing distributed on-site operations. In these environments, site engineers frequently pay out-of-pocket for local materials, fuel, and dynamic site services. However, Cendrol's traditional reimbursement pipeline was fragmented and highly manual.

I pitched a redesign of the expense reimbursement experience to simplify tracking, approvals, and workflows. The goal wasn't just to replace physical receipts with digital forms — it was to build a system that earned engineers' trust through speed, legibility, and zero friction.

02 /

THE CHALLENGE & OPERATIONAL FRICTION

Site engineers struggled to track where their submitted expenses stood, while the CFO and administrative teams were swamped by a lack of operational clarity:

  • Engineers struggled to trace submitted receipts, leading to repeated follow-up inquiries.
  • The legacy flow lacked visibility, making approvals feel completely disconnected from active site operations.
  • "but when we can't read the amount or the vendor name, what can we do?" — CFO (Chief Financial Officer).
  • Engineers felt mental stress over payout delays, which directly affected operational efficiency and trust.
03 /

USER RESEARCH & DESIGN TENSIONS

We embedded ourselves into site operations to map tensions and user expectations. The founder, Pooja Seth, noted: “Engineers struggled to track where reimbursements were getting delayed.”

During user studies, three primary tensions emerged:

  • Continuity vs Cognitive Overload: Engineers needed dynamic tracking updates without constant alerts distracting them from dangerous construction zones.
  • Legibility vs Data Depth: The administrative team required receipts, category codes, and timestamps, while site engineers required a 10-second submission flow.
  • Structural Clarity: Neglected areas and bottleneck stages needed to be visually prioritized so teams could identify delays instantly.
04 /

CORE PRODUCT FEATURES

Simplifying Submissions

Redesigned submission flows to minimize active input parameters and reduce operational friction during field requests.

Admin Approvals

Structured tracking rails, filtering parameters, and receipt layout matching to help CFOs approve claims in a single tap.

Structured Workflows

Clear status progression maps that highlight where claims stand, making the next steps immediate and legible.

Usability Signals

Structured visual hierarchy and color intensities that communicate recency, depth, and priority status automatically.

05 /

REFLECTIONS & IMPACT

This project proved that the best design stories aren't always about revolutionary visual interfaces. Sometimes, they are about taking a fragmented, stressful operational process and making it clear, legible, and structured.

By optimizing receipt submissions and tracking visibility, Cendrol Construct transformed how site engineering teams relate to expense management. The feedback from site testers after the beta launch was the ultimate indicator of success: “The system has helped me track and also record my spendings, and also this has helped us get reimbursements faster and efficiently.”

01

The Problem Space

UPSC aspirants face one of the world's most demanding examination ecosystems — 13 papers, 2+ years of preparation, over 10,000 pages of content, and a pass rate under 0.2%. Yet, the tools available are either static PDFs, unorganized YouTube playlists, or generic note apps with zero subject-awareness.

Through 12 interviews with active aspirants (ranging from first-timers to veterans), we found that the #1 failure point wasn't lack of effort — it was lack of structured direction. Students didn't know what to study, when to revise, and how to measure progress against the actual exam pattern.

89%felt overwhelmed by syllabus breadth
73%had no structured revision system
67%couldn't track weak topic areas
02

Research & Discovery

User Interviews: Conducted semi-structured 45-minute sessions with 12 aspirants across different preparation stages (Prelims, Mains, Interview). Identified 5 core pain-point archetypes: "The Scattered Studier", "The Over-Planner", "The Anxious Tracker", "The Content Hoarder", and "The Isolated Learner."

Competitive Analysis: Audited 8 existing EdTech platforms (BYJU's, Unacademy, Vision IAS app, ForumIAS, Drishti, etc.) focusing on IA quality, personalization depth, and content discoverability. Key gap: none offered AI-driven adaptive scheduling specific to UPSC's unique multi-paper, multi-year structure.

Heuristic Evaluation: Applied Nielsen's 10 heuristics to the top 3 competitors, identifying critical usability issues around navigation overwhelm, inconsistent information hierarchy, and poor error recovery in mock test flows.

03

Design Process & IA

Information Architecture: Designed a flat, 3-level IA to eliminate deep navigation hierarchies. Core structure: Home (Today's Plan + Streak) → Subjects (13-paper tree) → Topic (Deep dive + Quizzes) → Analytics (Performance dashboard).

Design System: Built a custom token-based system with UPSC-specific vocabulary: Aware / Understood / Mastered (instead of generic progress). Color coded: Warm Amber for Indian History, Deep Teal for Polity, Forest Green for Environment — providing instant subject-area recognition.

Wireframing to High Fidelity: Ran 3 wire-frame testing rounds with 6 aspirants each. Key iteration: removed the tab bar after Round 1 (aspirants were ignoring it) and replaced with a card-based home that consolidated all entry points into a single glanceable dashboard.

04

Key Features & Interactions

🧠 AI Study Planner

Dynamic schedule generation from exam date, current syllabus completion, and past performance data.

📖 Layered Content

Scan Cards → Summary → Deep Dive with Previous Year Questions — three reading depths per topic.

🔄 Spaced Repetition

Auto-scheduled revision reminders based on the forgetting curve — intelligently timed before scores decay.

📊 Analytics Hub

360° performance dashboard with topic heat maps, mock test trends, and peer benchmarking.

📰 Daily Current Affairs

AI-curated daily news digest mapped to UPSC syllabus tags — so nothing irrelevant makes it in.

🤝 Study Groups

Peer discussion pods organized by paper and preparation stage for contextual collaborative learning.

05

Outcomes & Reflections

What Worked: The 3-tier mastery system (Aware → Understood → Mastered) tested extremely well — aspirants found it more motivating than a generic progress bar because it reflected actual learning depth, not just time spent.

What I'd Change: The Study Groups feature wasn't tested thoroughly enough in Round 2 — aspirants loved the idea but were worried about noise from unmoderated discussions. A structured facilitated discussion model would be a stronger V2 direction.

The Biggest Learning: EdTech for high-stakes exams requires different design principles than consumer apps. Aspirants want efficiency over delight. Every micro-animation we added had to justify itself by saving cognitive load, not just looking pretty.
