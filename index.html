NETFLIX

SHOPPING & FAN COMMUNITY

MY ROLE Product Design Lead — Interactions, User Research, Prototyping
TEAM Shilpa (QA), Sharan (Visual Design), Gokul S Kaimal (UX)
DURATION April - May, 2024
OVERVIEW Bridging entertainment and shopping. Allows fans to buy exclusive merch while watching and share reviews.

01 / THE BACKSTORY

It all started with a simple idea: What if Netflix fans could buy exclusive merchandise from their favorite shows while watching? And why not make it even more engaging by adding a community space where fans could share reviews and interact with other fans and brands?

I pitched the idea, and over the course of a few weeks, I designed and built a prototype of the feature. After running several A/B tests to gather feedback, it became clear — this idea could truly elevate the Netflix experience.

Though the feature isn't live yet, the tests showed promising results, with users loving the concept of seamless shopping and engaging with the Netflix community.

02 / THE CHALLENGE

Being a personal project, there were no extra resources — no free bandwidth in design or engineering since we were already swamped with regular work. But we didn’t let that stop us.

We worked on the project whenever we could — after hours, on weekends, and even during hackathons. The passion to bring this idea to life kept us going, and after several weeks of design, prototyping, and A/B testing, the concept was finally taking shape.

"Bridging Entertainment and Shopping: A Seamless Experience for Fans to Connect, Shop, and Share."

03 / CORE FEATURES

In-App Marketplace

Integrated a seamless shopping experience, allowing users to browse and purchase merchandise related to the shows they’re watching.

Fan Interaction Space

Developed a community platform where users could share reviews, join discussions, and connect over their favorite shows and products.

Exclusive Merch Drops

Added the ability to release limited-edition items and exclusive show merchandise, creating excitement among fans.

Show-Specific Links

Enabled clickable links within shows to allow users to purchase products directly from scenes they’re watching.

04 / REFLECTIONS

To be honest, it's challenging to step outside of your regular duties and create something purely out of passion. But I'm proud of what we've conceptualized in this project, and it’s been an exciting journey of creativity and innovation. While this is still a personal project for now, I’m thrilled about the possibilities.

MOBILE CRM

CONSTRUCTION FIELD SYSTEM

MY ROLE Product Design Lead — Interactions, User Flows, Prototyping
TEAM Pallav Prathap (PM), Shrey Kansal (VD), Gokul S Kaimal (UX)
DURATION April - May, 2024
OVERVIEW A construction site mobile-first CRM tailored for marketing professionals traveling for face-to-face client meetings.

01 / THE BACKSTORY

Over the past few years, our client’s CRM has evolved into a comprehensive tool for the marketing team. Features like meeting tracking, task management, and client communication were introduced to boost efficiency.

The reason for this mobile-first design was that the marketing team frequently traveled for face-to-face client meetings, which kept them away from their office systems. The mobile CRM allowed them to stay productive on the go.

02 / THE CHALLENGE

The marketing team relied heavily on meetings, tasks, and communication. However, most CRM systems still required navigating through multiple screens, leading to inefficiencies. Over 60% of the team struggled with navigation, and 15% faced difficulties completing essential tasks.

Mobile-first solutions existed, but they didn’t fully meet the team's needs. Even though our CRM was optimized for mobile, its adoption was as low as 1.5% due to limited customization and user experience issues. Adoption is based on trust – we had to build that trust in the simplest, most efficient way possible.

03 / THE APPROACH

Our research team conducted a market study and user immersion sessions where we spoke to marketing professionals about their pain points. We discovered that marketing teams were already comfortable using simple tools like WhatsApp/email, and were hesitant to adopt complex new systems.

One thing became clear: convenience didn’t just mean reducing friction, but also understanding what the users felt comfortable with. We decided to go ahead with separate but integrated features rather than a single unified dashboard, which proved more intuitive for field technicians.

04 / CORE FEATURES

Meeting Tracking

Allows the marketing team to easily view assigned meetings, mark them as "done" (with supporting proof), and take notes – all in one place.

Task Management

Smooth task handling on the go. Technicians can manage follow-ups via WhatsApp, email, or instant client messages seamlessly.

Instant Updates

Real-time data synchronization. No lag, no loading screens in front of clients – just fast, reliable CRM data.

Field-Friendly Access

Mobile-first navigation system optimized for use in busy construction sites and outdoor client facilities.

05 / REFLECTIONS

This project had a lot of moving parts and stakeholders, each with their own perspectives. It wasn't just about designing a single feature—it was about crafting a system that could scale, evolve, and integrate seamlessly with the team’s ever-changing needs. The key? Keeping an eye on the big picture, knowing when to zoom in on details, and knowing when to reach out to the right people at the right time.

DESIGNERGRAM

SOCIAL UX & CREATIVE SPACE

MY ROLE Product Design Lead — UX Architecture, Interface Systems, Prototyping
TEAM Shilpa (QA), Sharan (Visual Design), Gokul S Kaimal (UX)
DURATION April - May, 2024
OVERVIEW A new-dimension social space tailored for designers to showcase journeys, snippets, and creative dimensions.

01 / THE CHALLENGE

Designergram was born from a simple insight: designers and creatives deserve a beautiful, mobile-first space that is not just a static grid, but a living diary of their design journey. A space where we can share brief snippets, follow trending layouts, and interact in a high-fidelity visual dimension.

Turn your design journey into a new dimension. Dive into a realm of endless possibilities, where your imagination knows no bounds.

02 / OBJECTIVES

🚀 Easy Onboarding

Simplify the process, reduce steps, and remove all points of user friction.

✨ Dash of Fun

Introduce micro-interactions that make users feel like they're engaging with something smart and playful.

🏄 Streamline Flow

Make authentication as smooth as possible – fast, efficient, and secure without roadblocks.

🔒 Solid Security

Accomplish all the above without compromising the app's rigorous security compliance.

03 / PROCESS & DESIGN

Wireframing and Prototyping: Started with low-fi wireframes to lay out the blueprint for the onboarding paths. After a few iterations, I created a high-fidelity prototype that was incredibly smooth.

UI Redesign: With wireframes validated, I dove into UI design, making sure every visual element was polished and perfectly aligned with the app's modern aesthetic.

Micro-interactions: Added mini-animations – things like interactive progress bars, loading indicators, and button feedback – so the app would feel more responsive. No more lifeless forms!

04 / REFLECTIONS

Efficiency + Engagement: Security doesn’t have to feel like a chore. A smooth, engaging experience can be just as secure.

Micro-interactions = Magic: Small details make a big impact. Users are much happier when they get that little bit of positive feedback!

UX Iteration is Key: The testing phase was vital – getting user feedback early and often kept the project on track.
